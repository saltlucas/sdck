How clean is your watershed?

 

</span> <br>Our education programs dont just get our kids to understand water science and remember facts. Were building an generation of future leaders armed with a passion for problem solving, deep respect for our shared waters and the knowledge and sense of responsibility that inspires a lifetime of action.<br><br>

 

                                                Its hard work, but the future of our water depends on it. We reach kids (and teachers, parents and caregivers) from every avenue we can with different modalities, immersive experiences and data-backed education strategies. Then we test, <a href="http://www.sdcoastkeeper.org/blog/environmental-education/project-swell-well-equipped-to-educate-future-generations" style="color:#029ee0;"><strong>measure the results</strong></a>, improve our approach and repeat. <br><br>

 

                                                Take a look at <a href="projectswell.org" style="color: #029ee0;"><strong> Project SWELL</strong></a>, it infuses hands-on education about San Diegos water issues into every corner of the classroom. Instead of sacrificing English class for science lessons, Project SWELL brings pollution terms to spelling tests. Our water supply lessons double as multiplication table practice and our entire curriculum helps teachers meet state benchmarks, ensuring its adopted enthusiastically across San Diego.

<br><br>

Last year was one of the most successful yet for Project SWELL. From August 2016 to April 2017, San Diego Coastkeeper:

<ul>

    <li>Educated more than 3,450 K-6th grade students about water science, pollution prevention and environmental stewardship through hands-on lesson plans<br><br></li>

    <li>Engaged more than 145 teachers with Project SWELL professional development<br><br></li>

    <li>Visited 63 schools within San Diego Unified School District to provide Project SWELL standard-aligned water science curriculum</li>

                                                </ul>

 

We continue providing free hands-on water science through Project SWELL in San Diego Unified schools thanks to the support of the Stiefel Behner Charitable Fund and our partners City of San Diego Think Blue and San Diego Unified School District.

The watershed plays a key role in San Diego Countys environmental health and has several significant pollution issues, but until now, we havent had the resources to include it in our regular monitoring program.<br><br>

 

These volunteers cared about their watershed. They told us they were ready to do whatever necessary to bring Agua Hedionda Creek into our monitoring program. Working together with San Diego Coastkeeper, they spent their nights and weekends creating a budget, building a scientific monitoring plan and raising the funds to pay for it all. Next month, youll see the data from Agua Hedionda Creek on our online water quality map.
